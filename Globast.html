Project Overview

“Globast” is an application which has a main focus on providing users a great movie watching experience regardless of their abilities.This application delves into improvements and innovative strategies to make them accessible for people with visual auditory, physical and cognitive impairments

Problem

  • Visual Impairment:Individuals with visual impairments, reading on-screen content due to blurry vision, difficulty distinguishing colors or contrast, or trouble perceiving the layout and functionality of buttons and menus. Additionally, the dialect or the computerized descriptions which describes the non-verbal affects their movie watching experience.
  • Auditory Impairment:Individuals with auditory impairments and who doesn’t have fluency in English can’t experience other language movies with English subtitles, following dialogue and sound effects through captions can be challenging. This makes them spend a lot of time in reading and understanding the subtitles rather than enjoying it.
  • Physical Impairment: Physical conditions like arthritis, tremors, or carpal tunnel syndrome using a mouse, keyboard, or touchscreen can be difficult for users and also difficult in navigating through the platform before or while watching their desired movies. Touchscreens can be challenging for users with limited hand-eye coordination.
  • Cognitive Impairment:People with cognitive impairments can’t take a lot of information all at once, which makes them feel overwhelmed for completing a specific task and understanding complex instructions and processing information overload can be difficult. As well as keeping up with the subtitles for other language movies which is very small and also with a fast paced scene.

Solution

  • Visual Impairment:For users with visual impairments, Globast provides high contrast design with the ability to resize text. Integration with screen readers allows content to be vocalized, while image descriptions and non-verbal cues also allows users to adjust these audio descriptions to their desired character voice.
  • Auditory Impairment:For users with auditory impairments, Globast provides advanced volume controls allow users with hearing loss to amplify sound. Additionally closed captions and subtitles, with customization options for size, color, and font and changing the subtitles to their desired language is also added as a new feature.
  • Physical Impairment: For users with Physical impairments Globast offers voice control, allowing users to navigate and control playback with voice commands. For users with limited mobility, keyboard shortcuts provide alternative navigation methods and also UI elements are designed with larger size and spacing for easier interaction.
  • Cognitive Impairment:For Cognitive impairments, Globast maintains a clean and uncluttered interface with clear and concise labels with a consistent layout with logical and categorized organisation to reduces confusion and make browsing effortless. It also comes up with highlighting the specific word in the captions which doesn’t allow their concentration to scatter.

Research Process

Personas & Scenarios were developed to understand the diverse needs of potential users. These personas included Victor, a screenwriter with quadriplegia; Charles, a user with tunnel vision impairment; Yue, an entrepreneur with language preferences; and Mush, a dyslexic film journalist. These personas helped shape the design process.

Low Fidelity

The low-fidelity phase involved creating a paper prototype for the application. This prototype outlined the basic structure, user interface, and key features. The initial design focused on inclusivity, incorporating features like audio descriptions, subtitles, voice assistance, and blocked captions. This phase allowed for quick iterations and adjustments based on feedback.

Hi-Fidelity

The high-fidelity prototype evolved from the low-fidelity version, incorporating detailed design elements, color schemes, and user interface refinements. The design adhered to inclusive principles, ensuring that each feature was carefully implemented to cater to users with visual, auditory, physical, and cognitive impairments.

User Testing

User testing was conducted with 4 participants (visually and auditory impaired) to understand how well they interact with Globast, an inclusive movie streaming platform.


The testers were given 5 tasks to complete:


  • Find and play "The Batman" movie in the superhero genre.

  • Find and play "The Batman" movie with Chinese subtitles.

  • See the cast and crew for "The Batman" movie.

  • Change the audio description voice for "The Batman" to sound like Robert Pattinson.

  • Switch user profiles from Sherlock to Heisenberg.

The time to complete each task was recorded, and testers were asked for their feedback on the platform.After the main testing, there was an engagement usability test where participants could explore the platform freely and provide feedback on how engaging it was. They were given a questionnaire to fill out at the end.


Iterations

Iteration 1

1. The scroll-to-top button was removed because it hid content sometimes. A sticky header was added instead, which stays visible as the user scrolls.

Iteration 2

2. The way to select subtitle language and audio description voice was changed. Originally, users had to scroll through a list to find their preference. Now, they can type their preference into a search bar for faster selection.
