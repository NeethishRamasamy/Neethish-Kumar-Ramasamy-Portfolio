Design for Understanding

Enhancing Elderly Engagement with Digital Terms & Conditions

Year

2024

Project

UX Research

Tools

Figma, Excel, Google forms, Google meet

Project Overview

This study explored methods to enhance the engagement and comprehension of digital Terms and Conditions (T&C) among elderly individuals in India. It investigated the reasons behind the gap between older adults and digital technologies, focusing on privacy concerns as a key barrier.

Problem

The study highlights the challenges elderly users face in navigating the complexity and accessibility of Terms & Conditions (T&Cs), which are crucial for informed decision-making and those significant hurdles are:

  • • Current T&Cs are often filled with complex legal language, making them difficult for anyone, especially older adults, to understand.
  • • Many elderly users experience vision problems. The current format of T&Cs, often lengthy and dense with text, can make them difficult to read.
  • • The sheer length and dry presentation of T&Cs can be disengaging, leading users to skip them entirely, potentially putting themselves at risk.
Solution

The study proposes a multi-pronged approach to make the elderly face the hurdles which they face in navigating the Terms & Conditions (T&Cs), they are:

  • • Simplifying the language, using clear formatting, and incorporating visuals like icons or flowcharts can make them easier to understand.
  • • Additionally, summaries of key points and the ability to customize settings can further empower users to make decisions wisely.
  • • Creating user-friendly T&Cs, Educating users about commonly used legal terms, ensuring transparency and protecting user rights, further helps in empowering elderly users online.

Research process

Literature Review

As a initial stage of research a literature analysis was conducted, to understand the reasons behind the gap between older adults and digital technologies which then narrowed down to three key concerns that can act as barriers:

  • The Digital Divide:This refers to the lack of access, skills, or knowledge to effectively use digital tools and they may also face challenges due to limited internet connectivity, affordability of devices, or a lack of training opportunities.
  • Psychological Hurdles:Beyond technical limitations, psychological factors can also play a role such as Fear of failure, a perceived lack of technical ability, or a sense of being overwhelmed by the constant technological advancements.
  • Privacy Concerns:Not Understanding how their data is collected and used can be a significant hurdle to adopting new technologies which leads to Data security and privacy concerns who are less familiar with online threats.

Survey

From these three factors Privacy concerns has been considered as the major issue by most of the literature papers which was analysed and a survey which was named as “Senior Digital Inclusion & Security Survey” was conducted targeting the Indian elderly demographic to understand their privacy concerns, technological challenges, and their preferences.

Design Process


From the survey, it has been found out that the older adults have significant privacy fears regarding digital technologies, including identity theft, data breaches, and financial loss. Despite of this either happening to them or hearing this news, they often neglect to read T&Cs that could possibly explain the future risks. Based on this, three prototypes for payment gateway app named Gpay, a commonly used app among participants (which was known by interviewing them online) using Figma. Before testing, a survey was again conducted to ensure that they are familiar with Gpay. Finally, researchers presented the prototypes to participants via WhatsApp links and Google Meet video calls.

User Testing

Pilot Testing

The pilot testing stage aimed to identify and address any initial usability issues with the prototypes before presenting them to a wider audience. Here's a closer look:

  • • Sample Size:A small group of participants was chosen for the pilot test. This allows for focused observation and detailed feedback on each user's interaction with the prototypes.
  • • Focus:The primary goal was to assess usability and observed how participants navigated the prototypes, identified any confusing elements, and ensured smooth functionality and also to identify any issues.
  • • Outcome:The pilot test revealed a single issue, that the background color of Prototype 2 was found to be distracting and impacted the readability and based on the feedback it was then changed from Yellow (#FDBD00) to a more user-friendly peach (#F3CEB1).
Actual Testing

Following the pilot test, the research shifted to more comprehensive user testing phase:

  • • Sample Size:With a group of fifteen participants, the updated prototypes were tested to know how the user experiences and perspectives regarding the T&Cs of the app.
  • • Individual Sessions: Unlike the pilot test, user testing was conducted individually via Google Meet. This led to tailor the testing experience to each participant's needs and preferences, leading to richer data.
  • • Data Collection:During each testing session the audio was recorded instead of video since few of the participants were not comfortable in recording video. Analyzing these recordings can revealed user hesitations, thought patterns, and emotional responses to the prototypes, providing valuable feedback beyond what simple observations can captured to gain deeper insights into user behaviour and thought processes.

Qualitative analysis

After the testing the participants were asked few questions and based on those answers, the qualitative analysis was done using Thematic Analysis method. The questions are as follows:

  • • Do you usually read the Terms and Conditions (T&C) before starting using apps or websites? 
  • • What factors influence you to read the T&Cs more or less? 
  • • What was your feeling about the length of each T&C format? 
  • • Were you able to understand the information given in the T&Cs?  
  • • If you knew that our data is being used for third party service enhancement would you continue using the app?  
  • • Would you be inclined to read T&Cs if they were simpler and shorter to understand? 
  • • Would you usually click on any of the sub-links within the T&Cs? If not, why? 
  • • Any suggestions that would make you more likely to read sub-links within T&Cs? 
  • • Any feedback or suggestions from your side to improve the T&Cs pages? 

Quantitative analysis

The quantitative analysis is done by measuring the time taken by each participant while testing and also by asking them to rate each prototype out of 5 based on readability and engagement using Likert scale rating method which was calculated and anlyzed by three different types using the SPSS software.

Descriptive Statistics


The primary objective of performing Descriptive Statistics is to get a basic understanding of distribution of time, engagement and readability ratings. The process and interpretation are:

  • • Mean and Median: Identities the average and middle value for the time spent, readability and engagement for each prototype.  
  • • Range and Standard Deviation: Used to understand the spread and variability of areas.  

This test reveals that prototype 3 has a higher mean value, indicating it is the best design for users. However, it is not statistically evident that prototype 2 is a good design, and Kruskal Wallis and Mann Whitney tests are performed to test the significance.

Kruskal-Wallis


The histogram graph for Readability and Engagement ratings indicates a need for a non-parametric test. The study has three designs, each rated by three participants. To confirm whether there is a statistically significant prototype, the Kruskal Wallis H test, a rank-based nonparametric test, is used. The results show a p value less than 0.05, confirming that there is a significant one in both readability and engagement ratings.

Mann- Whitney Test


To determine the most significant prototype, a post-hoc test was conducted using the Mann-Whitney U test. The results showed that design 3 was more engaging than design 1 & 2, design 2 & 3, and design 1 & 3. However, design 2 & 3 were not statistically significant enough to determine which was more engaging. In terms of readability, design 3 was found to be more effective than design 1 & 2, but design 2 was more effective. Overall, the comprehensive text combined with graphical elements was found to be easier to read for participants.

Key Findings

Qualitative analysis

  • • Disengagement Despite Concerns:People rarely engage with Terms & Conditions (T&C) due to their length, complex language, and lack of visual appeal. This surprising disconnect exists even though users’ express privacy concerns when using digital services.
  • • Readability Matters:People can’t engage with the content because of the size of the content makes them feel overwhelming and they feel stressed that they have to read so many while they scroll the whole page and see.
  • • Visual Influence:Content which is just put up in the page without any key elements to make them understand makes them feel bored and feel detached which causes the disengagement.
  • • User Coping Mechanisms:Faced with lengthy and complex T&Cs, users often develop strategies to cope with the information overload. These coping mechanism indirectly creates a trust on the software which makes them believe that they won’t be cheated just to cross the page by accepting it and then creates a fear later.
  • • Experiment Shows Positive Shift:Studies demonstrate that improved T&C presentation can lead to a more positive user perception of length. This could be because a well-designed T&C allows users to quickly grasp the information they need, potentially increasing their awareness of the information presented and making them feel more comfortable using the service.
Quantitative analysis

The key findings of the testing after analysing SPSS analysis data are:

  • • Central Tendency and Dispersion:The analysis of central tendency measures (mean, median, mode) indicated varying levels of time spent on each prototype, with prototype 1 taking the longest and prototype 2 the least. Measures of dispersion (range, variance, standard deviation) highlighted the spread of ratings, affecting the interpretation of average scores.
  • • Prototype Performance:Prototype 3 consistently received higher mean values for both readability and engagement ratings, indicating it was the most favored design among participants. The mean differences in ratings between prototypes 1 and 2 ranged from 1.5 to 1.8, while differences between prototypes 2 and 3 were lower, ranging from 1.2 to 1.3.
  • • Statistical Significance:The Kruskal-Wallis H test confirmed significant differences mong the three prototypes for both readability and engagement ratings, with p-values less than 0.05 indicating statistical significance. This test identified that at least one prototype differed significantly from the others in terms of user ratings.
  • • Pairwise Comparisons:Mann-Whitney U tests revealed significant differences in engagement ratings between designs 1 & 2 and designs 1 & 3, establishing that design 3 was significantly more engaging. However, no significant difference was found between designs 2 & 3, suggesting both were perceived as engaging by participants.
  • • Readability Insights:Readability ratings showed significant differences between all pairs of prototypes, with design 3 rated as the most readable. The combination of comprehensive text and graphical elements in design 3 contributed to its higher readability score, indicating a preference for this format among participants.

Conclusion

While investigating the gap between older adults and the digital devices and technologies , the study identified significant concerns about T&C complexity and lack of understanding among older adults, raising the question: "Why aren't people reading T&C pages?". The study found that lengthy, jargon-filled T&C pages contribute to older adults' avoidance of digital technologies. Simplifying the language, incorporating visuals, and maintaining a clear format can improve comprehension and engagement. However, the study acknowledges the limitations of a small participant pool and the need for further research.

While not directly addressing the initial question of bridging the entire digital technology gap, this study empowers older adults to make informed decisions through improved T&C accessibility. They can now consciously choose whether to use a service based on a clear understanding of its terms, reducing reliance on avoidance due to fear and confusion.
